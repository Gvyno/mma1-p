Situated on the top floor of a four-storey edifice dating back to 1870 and restored under the expertise of architect Antonio Alvarez Gil, the apartment is a true architectural gem combining industrial flair with authentic Andalusian charm. With an area of approximately 280 m2 and 3 levels, it occupies the entire floor and boasts a distinctive tower that once served as a functional lighthouse, now transformed into a private vantage point affording panoramic views of the city of Malaga. 

Upon crossing the threshold of this unparalleled dwelling, one is greeted by an impressive L-form living area featuring numerous window-doors and vaulted ceilings with wooden beams. This area graciously extends access to a charming inner patio with a dining area discreetly nestled beyond elegant glass doors. The open plan kitchen with a central island is equipped with top-quality appliances. The sleeping area encompasses three bedrooms, two of which are en suite, complemented by an additional shared bathroom. The master bedroom incorporates an ample walk-in closet. The wooden stairs lead to the second level comprised of a workspace and a storage room. The upper level reveals an observation lounge with stunning 360 views of Malaga. 

The property features outstanding qualities: floor structure reinforced with supporting concrete slab; interior walls made of newly built brick masonry; Lapacho wood flooring; wall coverings made with traditional stucco of the highest quality; solid stone cladding in bathroom walls from the Guijuelo quarry; original wooden ceilings of the building dated 1870 restored in 2005; Niko domotized lighting.

Situated in the heart of the historic centre, this property enjoys a close proximity to key points of interest, such as La Alcazaba, the Roman Theatre, Calle Larios and more.
